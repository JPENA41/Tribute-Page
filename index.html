Honoring Satoru Iwata

Satoru Iwata: A timeline

1959

Born in Sapporo, Japan

1978

He enlisted at the Tokyo Institute of Technology with a major in computer science and studied his first computer, a Commodore PET, by dismantling it.

1982

As the fifth full-time employee at HAL (a game development company), he helped reach a deal with Nintendo to produce games for the NES.

1983

As a president at HAL, he successfully recovered the company from its ¥1.5 billion debt within six years by reading books or taking guidance from skilled professionals.

1999

Tsunekazu Ishihara (president of the Pokémon Company) credited Iwata for popularizing Pokémon in the Western market. For example, he saved the development of Gold & Silver by allowing space for all of the data into the cartridges.

2002

After becoming Nintendo's fourth president, he met directly with the employees of various departments, breaking Yamauchi's (Nintendo's prior president) tradition of carrying only annual speech.

2004

Following the success of the handheld device, the Game Boy Advance (or GBA), Iwata presented the Nintendo DS, which came with two screen, one with touch enabled. It suppress the GBA thanks to Brain Age (trainning game series), which attracted non-gamers.

2006

To make games more accessible, Iwata developed a family-oriented home console called Wii. He ditched the standard controller and opted for a motion based controller, which became the Wii Remote.

2014

With Nintendo's profit margin declining and having two failed product at hand, Nintendo 3DS, released in 2011, and Wii U, Iwata took a 50% cut of his own salary and refused to firing his employers.

2015

Died at the age of 55 from bile duct cancer
